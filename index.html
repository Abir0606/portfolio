Advancing Low-Light Object Detection with YOLO Models

Second author

View Abstract

Low-light object detection is needed for ensuring security, enabling surveillance, and enhancing safety in diverse applications, including autonomous vehicles, surveil- lance systems, and search and rescue operations. This paper presents a compre- hensive study on low-light object detection using state-of-the-art You Only Look Once (YOLO) models, including YOLOv3, YOLOv5, YOLOv6, and YOLOv8, aim- ing to enhance detection performance under challenging low-light conditions. The ExDark dataset is a dataset that consists of adequate low-light images, modified to simulate realistic low-light scenarios, and employed for evaluation. The deep learning algorithm optimizes You Only Look Once’s architecture for low-light detec- tion by adapting the network structure and training strategies while preserving the algorithm’s integrity. This paper’s experimental results show that YOLOv8 consis- tently outperforms baseline models, achieving significant improvements in accuracy and robustness in low-light scenarios. The deep learning algorithm that acquired the best score, YOLOv8s, had an mAP score of 0.5513. This work contributes to the field of low-light object detection, offering promising solutions for real-world applica- tions like nighttime surveillance and autonomous navigation in low-light conditions, addressing the growing demand for advanced low-light object detection.

Detection of Brain Tumor using CNN Architecture

First author

View Abstract

Brain cancer is characterized by the unusual growth of cells in the brain. These can- cerous cells may either initiate in the brain itself (primary brain cancer) or migrate to the brain from other areas of the body (metastatic or secondary brain cancer). This atypical cell growth can manifest as a mass or tumour, potentially disrupting typical brain functions. Using modern machine learning technology brain cancer can be detected from 2D Magnetic Resonance brain Images (MRI) images. Such technologies are MobileNet-v2, Visual Geometry Group (VGG)-16 and Residual- Net(ResNet50) which will be used in this book chapter. The book chapter covers two distinct datasets: one that focuses on determining the presence or absence of tumors (binary dataset) and another that categorizes tumors into different classes (multiclass dataset). In this book chapter, we implied the Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model in the Python and TensorFlow environment. The primary objective of this book chapter is to differentiate between regular and irregular cells of certain parts of the brain, relying on features derived from texture and statistics

Banana Leaf Spot Disease Detection Using YOLO model

Second author

View Abstract

Banana leaf spot diseases such as Cordana, Pestalotiopsis, and Sigatoka pose significant challenges to the worldwide banana sector, limiting the supply of essential nutrients as well as the stability of the global economy. In this study, we utilize deep learning models to ensure the precise detection of these diseases in a proper way with better accuracy. Currently, detection methods for these diseases are often labor-intensive, time-consuming, and prone to inaccuracies. There is a significant gap in the development of efficient and precise detection techniques that can be readily deployed in the field. The importance of effective management techniques in ensuring food security and long-term livelihoods is emphasized by our research. With a subset of the Ba- nanaLSD dataset, the experimental findings with YOLOv8 show promising metrics: a precision rate of 88.9%, and a recall rate of 92%, for the YOLOv8s model. These results have the potential to significantly increase crop yields and allow for more accurate leaf spot disease detection in fields.

Particle Level Noise Removal Using Machine Learning and Graph Neural Networks: A Comparative Study

Second author

View Abstract

Particle physics has always been an intriguing topic for scientists in the research field. Exotic particles pave the way for the exploration of the early universe. In order to separate exotic particles from the noise, scientists have done much research in the field of particle-level noise removal. This study addresses the problem of particle-level noise removal. Traditional machine learning models have been able to mitigate particle-level noise but they are inefficient in terms of accuracy. The introduction of Graph Neural Network and its various models addresses the efficiency of particle-level noise removal. This research compares the traditional machine learning model, specifically XGBoost and Support Vector Machine(SVM) with Graph Attention Net- work(GAT) to find the difference in efficiency between them. The data which was used to compare these models were generated using PYTHIA 8.3. The models were hyper-tuned and trained on 10K proton-proton collisions. Where GAT has achieved 80.1% accuracy, while XGBoost and SVM have achieved 72.9% and 67.3% accuracy. After training the model it was evaluated using Explainable AI (XAI). XAI GraphLIME was used for node- specific explanation and SHAP for feature importance. After retraining GAT with the Extracted XAI features, it showed a huge improvement across the board with 94.8% accuracy.

Achieving High-Accuracy Human Activity Recognition Using BERTBased Classwise Ensemble Models

Second author

View Abstract

Human Activity Recognition (HAR) is a critical task in ubiquitous computing, offering numerous applications in healthcare, sports, and human-computer interaction. The study presents an innovative way of leveraging the BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) architecture to enhance the accuracy of HAR systems. By utilizing a classwise ensemble method, we effectively harness the capabilities of BERT for sequence classification, adapting it to the multi-dimensional time-series data characteristic of HAR tasks. Our model was trained and evaluated on the UCI Human Activity Recognition (HAR) dataset, which includes sensor data from accelerometers and gyroscopes which recorded activities like walking, walking upstairs, sitting, walking downstairs, sitting, standing and lying down from 30 participants. Our approach achieved outstanding performance through extensive experimentation, attaining 98% accuracy, 97% precision, 98% recall, and 97% F1-score. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the BERT-based classwise ensemble method to capture complex patterns of human activi- ties, outperforming traditional HAR models. This advancement paves the way for more accurate and reliable HAR systems, with significant implications for enhancing user experience in various application domains.
